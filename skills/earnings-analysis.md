# Quarterly Earnings Analysis

Follow this checklist when evaluating a company's most recent earnings data.

## Workflow Checklist

- [ ] **Revenue trajectory**: Compare revenue to prior periods. Is growth accelerating, decelerating, or flat? Flag any quarter where revenue declines while the company guides higher.

- [ ] **Earnings quality check**: Compare net income to operating cash flow. If net income consistently exceeds operating cash flow, earnings may be propped up by accruals or one-time gains. Healthy ratio: OCF >= 80% of net income over trailing 3 periods.

- [ ] **Margin analysis**: Calculate gross margin and net margin. Compare to the company's own 3-year average. A margin squeeze (current < average by >200bps) is a yellow flag -- identify whether it is temporary (input costs, investment phase) or structural (pricing power loss).

- [ ] **Free cash flow conversion**: Compute FCF / Net Income ratio. Strong businesses convert >70% of earnings to free cash flow. Below 50% signals heavy capex requirements or working capital drain.

- [ ] **Balance sheet snapshot**: Check current ratio (target >= 1.5 for non-financials) and debt-to-equity (target <= 1.0 for most sectors). Rising leverage quarter-over-quarter without corresponding revenue growth is a red flag.

- [ ] **Return on equity decomposition**: Is ROE driven by high margins (good), asset efficiency (good), or leverage (risky)? ROE above 15% is the Buffett threshold, but only if not leverage-driven.

- [ ] **Earnings surprise direction**: Did actuals beat or miss the pre-computed trend? A company that consistently beats its own trajectory by small amounts may be sandbagging (bullish management). Large misses in either direction signal forecast uncertainty.

- [ ] **Final signal**: Synthesize the above into bullish (earnings quality strong, margins stable/expanding, FCF healthy), bearish (earnings quality deteriorating, margins compressing, leverage rising), or neutral (mixed signals, insufficient data).
