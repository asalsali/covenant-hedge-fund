# Macro Regime Identification

Follow this checklist to determine the prevailing macro regime and position accordingly.

## Workflow Checklist

- [ ] **Trend alignment scan**: Check 50-day and 200-day moving averages (if available). Price above both = risk-on regime. Price below both = risk-off. Price between = transitional. A golden cross (50 > 200) or death cross (50 < 200) signals regime shift.

- [ ] **Volatility regime**: Assess recent daily volatility. Low vol (<1% daily) = complacency regime -- be alert for breakouts. High vol (>2.5% daily) = stress regime -- size positions smaller, expect mean reversion. Measure whether vol is expanding or contracting relative to recent history.

- [ ] **Momentum divergence**: Compare short-term price change (7d/30d) to long-term (200d). If short-term momentum is strongly positive while long-term is negative, this may be a bear market rally (Burry pattern). If both align positive, trend is confirmed.

- [ ] **Margin and leverage stress**: Look for margin compression in the underlying company or sector. Rising debt-to-equity combined with falling margins signals a potential credit event. This is the structural disconnect Burry looks for.

- [ ] **Innovation cycle positioning** (Wood lens): Is the company in an exponential adoption curve? Revenue growth rate >25% CAGR with earnings growth lagging = classic growth investment phase. Revenue growth decelerating below 15% = maturation, shift to profitability scrutiny.

- [ ] **Regime classification**: Based on the above, classify the current environment as one of:
  - **Risk-on expansion**: uptrend, low vol, positive momentum alignment
  - **Late cycle**: uptrend but rising vol, margin compression
  - **Correction**: downtrend, high vol, negative short-term momentum
  - **Bottoming**: downtrend but vol declining, short-term momentum turning positive

- [ ] **Position sizing implication**: Risk-on = full conviction sizing. Late cycle = reduce position size by 30-50%. Correction = defensive, only highest-conviction names. Bottoming = begin accumulating contrarian positions with small size.
